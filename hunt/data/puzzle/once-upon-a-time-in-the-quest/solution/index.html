Solvers visiting the Quest Coast are presented with a medallion, and told they will need to visit many wondrous realms.

On solving a puzzle in this round, solvers are told they need to complete a quest to a fantastical land, and are presented with a mysterious glyph to get them started.

Each glyph refers back to a puzzle in Round One or Round Two; the eight puzzle are all associated with imaginary lands from childrens' or YA literature, and each of these lands is named specifically in the puzzle’s title or flavor text. The medallion gives the initial letters of each of the lands, which serves as an ordering for the answers in this round.

The Enchanted Garden glyph solution
Dinotopia Glyph - Solution: LIFE
by Dan Lepage, Shai Nir Hana and Mark Halpin

This glyph calls back to the grid shapes in Dinotopia; the silohuetted T-Rex might also suggest this. The darkened pieces overlaid on the grid shapes form ominoes that can be rotated and repositioned to form the T-Rex shape. Dots within the T-Rex shape indicate the locations of footprint symbols in the shape when the ominoes are correctly positioned.

Rotating the ominoes will necessarily rotate the footprint symbols. In their new orientations, the symbols can be read in the strings withn the T-Rex as the clue AS IAN SAID, IT, UH, FINDS A WAY, a reference to Jurassic Park's Ian Malcolm's characterization of LIFE.

Something Command glyph solution
The Wonderful Wizard of Oz Glyph - Solution: KNIGHT
by joon pahk and Mark Halpin

This glyph calls back to the Rows Garden grid in The Wonderful Wizard of Oz. It suggests swapping the positions of the medium and light blooms. Those blooms pair uniquely, and, if all pairs swap, the new top and bottom rows spell the clue SMOTHERED MATE GIVER, and shown in the grid below.

In chess, the piece that delivers a "smothered mate" is the KNIGHT.

This glyph calls back to the "winter" Hashi / Bridges grid in The Wizard of Earthsea. It suggests eliminating the double bridges from that grid, and, if this is done, the remaining single bridges make letterforms spelling the answer CHAPEL, as shown below.

Royal Steeds glyph solution
Alice's Adventures in Wonderland Glyph - Solution: TENNESSEE
by Eric Berlin and Mark Halpin

This glyph calls back to the snaky grids in Alice's Adventures in Wonderland. The grids may be overlapped in the configuration shown (the curved ends disambiguate which is which). In the highlighted spaces, take the letter in either the shaded or unshaded space from the crossing tracks, as indicated by the glyph (in some cases, the spot in both tracks will be either shaded or unshaded, but in those cases the letter is identical). Reading by rows gives the answer TENNESSEE. (see diagram below).
