In Decrypto, two "miscommunications" ends the game -- in each case, the players lose in the fifth round. Correct gueses are shown, and miscommunicated clue words are shown in bold.

Solvers must work around the miscommunications and determine the four words in each round, which form the wordplay part of a cryptic clue. The number of hours is the enumeration of the answer.

The first letters of the misleading clues spells ANSWERS ARE PEN NAMES, and indeed that is the case:

The answers to the cryptic clues are pen names of famous writers:

  • BELL: BRONTE
  • BOX: VIDAL
  • CAREFUL: FRANKLIN
  • HOOKS: WATKINS
  • QUICK: KRENTZ
  • RICE: OBRIEN
  • SAND: DUPIN
  • TOMORROW: PERKINS
  • WEARY: GOREY

If you take the initial letters of the players of a given round, convert them to numbers via A=1, B=2, etc, and use those numbers to index into the writer's real names, you'll get a letter. These letters, in the order of the roster of agents, spells the answer to the puzzle, FABBRIZIO DISGUISEY.
