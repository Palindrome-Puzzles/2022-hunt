First solve the minesweeper/shakashaka grid based on the two puzzles' original rules and the extra rule given (that mines = shakashaka black squares).

An easy point to start is the 5 on the left side. Because shakashaka clues only go up to 4, we know this 5 has to be a clue for minesweeper, indicating that there are 5 mines around. No matter where the 5 mines are, the two 0's orthogonally adjacent to the 5 will see at least one mine, hence these two 0s cannot be minesweeper clues. They're therefore shakashaka clues/shakashaka squares/mines.

The white rectangles of shakashaka, with the exception of the 1x1 squares, cover some numbers. Sum up the numbers under each rectangle -- halving the numbers that are only partially covered by white triangles. Order by the rectangle's areas (which form a nice sequence of even numbers from 2 to 18). The sums in this order are 4, 9, 14, 7, 4, 15, 14, 7, 19, which translates (in A1Z26) to the answer DINGDONGS.
